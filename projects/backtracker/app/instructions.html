Instructions for Backtracker

The goal of this game is to try to find special objects by navigating through a tree "maze".

To play this game, you must first load a file from the menu on the right-hand side of the page.

You can then generate the tree by clicking on "leaf nodes", which are the discs at the bottom of the tree.

Orange discs may lead to a solution, red discs cannot lead to a solution, while green discs at the bottom of the tree are the objects we are interested in.

It is up to you to develop strategies to find the green nodes! We present some information about the objects being generated by giving the nodes different sizes, but the structure of the tree may give you other hints.

Start with small cases, trying to develop strategies before moving on to the harder large cases. If you are the first person to solve a particular case your name will be added to the honour roll!

Controls

  • Clicking on a leaf node will start the search program, which will explore a new part of the tree.
  • Clicking on the root node will move the whole tree down by one level.
  • Clicking on an internal node will move that node to the root.
  • Zoom in and out of the tree with + and - buttons.
  • Change the search depth by selecting the appropriate value in the menu.
  • Toggle between search mode and inspect mode. In search mode you can generate and explore the backtracking tree, while in inspect mode you can see what objects are being represented by nodes in the tree.

About

Foldit, combinatorial search - hard problem, humans although far slower than computers may be able to develop good heuristics to solve genuinely hard problems. When there are 10100 nodes in a tree, brains are more important than brawn.

Decomposition of Gram matrices - hard problem. Hadamard conjecture.

Backtracker - general paradigm for combinatorial search. This is just first application.
