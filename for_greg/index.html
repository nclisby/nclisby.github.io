Hi Sophia and Keith,

I'm just going to write briefly about some ideas for gemstone and mineral fossicking, which we might be able to do with the kids sometime.

I know Laney's really keen on this (she's mentioned it a couple of times, e.g. when I was in science class the week before last), and I know Maia is too, so it would be great if we could find a joint activity sometime.

I've been reading up and collecting resources on places to fossick near Melbourne, and unfortunately there is not terribly much - or at least there are no easy places to find large gemstones nearby.

The best places seem to be various creeks in state parks and forests where fossicking for gemstones is allowed. In particular, Maia and I went up to a creek near Daylesford about a month and a half ago to fossick for zircons. This didn't turn out very well! Maia had alarmingly little patience, and it was quite cold, and in the end we found about 20 specks of colour varying in size from microscopic to very small. My understanding is that you can get occasional gem quality zircons from this area, and if we had had a little bit more patience (only spent 30 minutes there) and tried a different spot or two we would likely have fared a bit better.

I didn't tell you about this at the time because I was not at all confident we'd find anything at all - I wanted to give it a try once, and then organise a joint trip the next time around.

So, Daylesford is an option for a joint trip, provided you have low expectations! Maybe sometime in week 2 of the school holidays, or possibly as soon as next weekend, if you're up for it.

Another option, and one I'm seriously considering doing with Maia this week, is to visit the Mt Shadwell quarry near Mortlake, which is about 2.5 hours drive from Melbourne. There they have plentiful olivine scoria "bombs" which you can crack open with a hammer to find crystals, and in rare cases peridot gemstones. The quarry is open for fossicking on Fridays only, so I'm thinking of going this coming Friday, 25/9 as we will be away on the following week. Laney suggested that you'd be away or busy during week 1, so this might not be an option for you.

I'm considering whether to head to Colac the night before and staying in a motel, or just going directly to Mortlake and back on the same day.

I have to admit, I'm nervous that Maia will decide it's too much hard work and not engage with it. So, it could either be lots of fun or a complete disaster of a trip.

To see what sort of minerals can be found have a look at: https://www.flickr.com/photos/jeremypike/sets/72157650783884118 , especially down the bottom of the page.

To crack open the scoria you will need a hammer - I have a so-called "crack hammer" for cracking open rocks - but a sledge hammer would work and might even be better, sturdy shoes, and working gloves for handling the rocks.

The Mortland shire page is: http://www.moyne.vic.gov.au/Our-Services/Mount-Shadwell-Quarry It's necessary to fill in an indemnity form which can be sent to the council via email, and I have confirmed that the quarry is open for fossicking this coming Friday.

OK, I'll leave it there for now. Please let me know if you're keen on a trip to Mt Shadwell quarry or Daylesford anytime soon.

Cheers,

Nathan
