Schedule

A provisional version of the schedule is available below (pdf version here [1]). All abstracts which have been received so far have been posted to the abstracts [2] page.

Monday

8:30am Registration
9:00am Jon Machta - Parallel Tempering and Population Annealing for Rough Free Energy Landscapes [3]
9:45am David Yllanes - The low-temperature phase of Ising spin glasses: parallel-tempering simulations with sample-dependent thermalisation on the Janus computer [4]
10:10am Yukito Iba - Rare Event Sampling using Multicanonical Monte Carlo [5]
10:35am Morning tea / Registration
11:10am Peter Young - Monte Carlo Simulations of Heisenberg Spin Glasses [6]
11:55am Piotr Kowalczyk - Microscopic model of carbonaceous nanoporous molecular sieves - anomalous transport in molecularly confined spaces [7]
12:20pm Michael Bachmann - Hierarchical Subphase Transitions in Molecular First-Order Nucleation Processes [8]
12:45pm Lunch
2:00pm Victor Martín-Mayor - Monte Carlo simulations of (quasi) constrained ensembles [9]
2:25pm  Beatriz Seoane - Effective potential study of hard-spheres crystallization [10]
3:10pm Gerd Schröder-Turk - Hard Sphere Liquids in Complex Pores [11]
3:35pm Afternoon tea
4:00pm Alan Sokal - Overcoming critical slowing-down: Where do we stand 23 years after Swendsen and Wang? [12]
5:00pm Finish

Tuesday

9:00am  Wolfhard Janke - Error Estimation and Reduction with Cross Correlations [13]
9:45am  Ebrahim Foulaadvand - Optimisation of quantum Monte Carlo wave function: steepest descent method [14]
10:10am  Manolo Per - Efficient calculation of unbiased estimates in diffusion quantum Monte Carlo [15]
10:35am  Morning tea
11:10am  Henk Blöte - Potts models with long-ranged interactions [16]
11:55am  Munetake Sasaki - Stochastic Cutoff Method for Long-Range Interacting Systems [17]
12:20pm  Peter Anders - Diagrammatic Monte Carlo method for bosonic impurity problems [18]
12:45pm  Lunch
2:00pm  Jesus Salas - Dynamic critical behavior of the Wang-Swendsen-Kotecky algorithm for two-dimensional Potts antiferromagnets [19]
2:45pm  Elmar Bittner - Replica-Exchange Cluster Algorithm [20]
3:10pm  Hidemaro Suwa - Markov Chain Monte Carlo without Detailed Balance and Bounce-free Worm Algorithm [21]
3:35pm  Afternoon tea
4:00pm  David Landau - Exploring Complex Free Energy Landscapes with Wang-Landau Sampling
5:00pm  Finish
7:00pm  Dinner at Bokchoy Tang

Wednesday

9:00am  Ian Snook - The observation of formation and annihilation of solitons and standing strainwave superstructures in a two-dimensional colloidal crystal [22]
9:45am  George Opletal - Hybrid Reverse Monte Carlo Modelling of Disordered Materials [23]
10:10am  Peter Zoontjens - Kinetic Monte Carlo Modelling of Pt on Au (111) in Bimetallic Catalysis [24]
10:35am  Morning tea
11:15am  Thomas Prellberg - PERM and all that - a comparison of growth algorithms [25]
12:00pm  Ofer Biham - Reaction networks with fluctuations: from inter-stellar chemistry to intra-cellular biology [26]
12:45pm  Lunch
2:00pm  Martin Weigel - Simulating spin models on GPU [27]
2:45pm Phani Nukala - Low-rank updates in statistical physics applications [28]
3:10pm  Wenan Guo - A worm simulation of the loop model on the square lattice [29]
3:35pm  Afternoon tea
4:00pm  Werner Krauth - The event-chain algorithm and the melting transition for two-dimensional hard spheres [30]
4:45pm  Finish


26 – 28 July, 2010, Melbourne, Australia.
